Objective Functions¶

Costs¶

The objective function of the model is to minimize the net present value of the system's cost. This includes investment cost, fixed O&M cost, variable cost and fuel cost by cost type, technology cost, transmission line cost by the source of cost, and operation cost and planning cost by the source of cost.

The cost equations are defined as follows:

\[\begin{split}\rm{cost} &= \rm{cost}_\rm{tech}^\rm{var} + \rm{cost}_\rm{line}^\rm{var} + \rm{cost}^\rm{fuel} + \rm{cost}_\rm{tech}^\rm{fix} + \rm{cost}_\rm{line}^\rm{fix} + \rm{cost}_\rm{tech}^\rm{inv} + \rm{cost}_\rm{line}^\rm{inv} \\ \\ \rm{cost}_\rm{tech}^\rm{var} &= \frac{\sum_{t,m,y,z,\rm{te}}C_{y,z,\rm{te}}^\rm{tech-var}\times \rm{gen}_{t,m,y,z,\rm{te}}}\rm{Weight} \times \rm{factor}_{y}^\rm{var} \\ \\ \rm{cost}_\rm{line}^\rm{var} &= \frac{\sum_{t,m,y,z_s,z_o}C_{y,z}^\rm{line-var}\times \rm{export}_{t,m,y,z_s,z_o}}\rm{Weight} \times \rm{factor}_{y}^\rm{var} \\ \\ \rm{cost}^\rm{fuel} & = \frac{\sum_{t,m,y,z,\rm{te}}C_{y,z,\rm{te}}^\rm{fuel}\times \rm{gen}_{t,m,y,z,\rm{te}}}\rm{Weight} \times \rm{factor}_{y}^\rm{var} \\ \\ \rm{cost}_\rm{tech}^\rm{fix} &= \sum_{y,z,\rm{te}}C_{y,z,\rm{te}}^\rm{tech-fix}\times \rm{cap}_{y,z,\rm{te}}^\rm{existing-tech}\times \rm{factor}_{y}^\rm{fix} \\ \\ \rm{cost}_\rm{line}^\rm{fix} &= \sum_{y,z_s,z_o}C_{y,z_s,z_o}^\rm{line-fix}\times \rm{cap}_{y,z_s,z_o}^\rm{existing-line}\times \rm{factor}_{y}^\rm{fix} \\ \\ \rm{cost}_\rm{tech}^\rm{inv} &= \sum_{y,z,\rm{te}}C_{y,z,\rm{te}}^\rm{tech-inv}\times \rm{cap}_{y,z,\rm{te}}^\rm{tech-inv}\times \rm{factor}_{y}^\rm{inv} \\ \\ \rm{cost}_\rm{line}^\rm{inv} &= \sum_{y,z_s,z_o}C_{y,z_s,z_o}^\rm{line-inv}\times \rm{cap}_{y,z_s,z_o}^\rm{line-inv}\times \rm{factor}_{y}^\rm{inv} \times 0.5 \\ \\\end{split}\]

Factors¶

To account for the variable factor, fixed factor, and investment factor, we need to convert all future costs to their net present value. This means adjusting for the time value of money so that all costs are expressed in terms of today's dollars.

We also assume that variable cost and fixed cost for non-modelled years are assumed to be equal to the cost of the last modelled year preceding them. This allows for consistent comparison across different time periods and technologies.

Variable Factor

Given the following:

  • Variable cost of modeled year: \(B\)

  • Discount rate: \(r\)

  • \(m\)-th modeled year: \(m = y - y_\text{min}\)

  • Depreciation periods: \(n\)

The total present value can be calculated as follows:

\[\begin{split}\begin{align*} \text{total present value} &= \frac{B}{(1+r)^m} + \frac{B}{(1+r)^{m+1}} + \cdots + \frac{B}{(1+r)^{(m+k-1)}} \\ \\ &= B(1+r)^{(1-m)}\frac{1-(1+r)^k}{r} \\ \\ \end{align*}\end{split}\]

And we can calculate the variable factor as follows:

\[\begin{split}\begin{align*} \text{factor}_{y}^{var} &= (1+r)^{1-m_y}\frac{1-(1+r)^{k_y}}{r} \\ \\ m_{y} &= y - y_\text{min} \\ \\ k_{y} &= y_\text{periods} \\ \\ \end{align*}\end{split}\]

Fixed Factor

We can equate the fixed factor with the variable factor as follows:

\[\text{factor}_{y}^\text{fix} = factor_{y}^\text{var}\]

Investment Factor

Given the following:

  • Weighted Average Cost of Capital (WACC, or otherwise known as the interest rate): \(i\)

  • Discount rate: \(r\)

  • \(m\)-th modeled year: \(m = y - y_\text{min}\)

  • Length of \(m\)-th planning periods: \(k\)

The total present value can be calculated as follows:

\[\begin{split}\begin{align*} \text{total present value} &= \frac{P}{(1+r)^m} \\ \\ &= \frac{\frac{A}{(1+i)} + \frac{A}{(1+i)^2} + \cdots + \frac{A}{(1+i)^n}}{(1+r)^m} \\ \\ &= A\frac{1-(1+i)^{-n}}{i}\times\frac{1}{(1+r)^m} \\ \\ \end{align*}\end{split}\]

From the above, we can solve for the annualized cost of depreciation periods, \(A\), as:

\[\begin{split}A = P\frac{i}{1-(1+i)^{-n}} \\ \\\end{split}\]

The capital recovery factor is then calculated as:

\[\begin{split}\text{capital recovery factor} = \frac{i}{1-(1+i)^{-n}} \\ \\\end{split}\]

Let's focus on the time periods that fall within the modelled time horizon (indicated in black colour). We can calculate the length of time periods, \(k\), as follows:

\[\begin{split}k = y_{max} - y \\ \\\end{split}\]

Using \(k\), we can calculate the net present value as follows:

\[\begin{split}\text{net present value} = \begin{cases} \frac{\frac{A}{(1+r)} + \frac{A}{(1+r)^2} + \cdots + \frac{A}{(1+r)^{min(n, k)}}}{(1+r)^m} & \text{if }n \le k \\ \\ \text{total present value} & \text{if }n > k \\ \\ \frac{A\frac{1-(1+r)^{-k}}{r}}{(1+r)^m} = P\frac{i}{1-(1+i)^{-n}}\times\frac{1-(1+r)^{-k}}{r(1+r)^m} & \text{otherwise} \\ \\ \end{cases}\end{split}\]

And we can calculate the investment factor as follows:

\[\begin{split}factor_{y}^{inv} = \frac{i}{1-(1+i)^{-n}}\times\frac{1-(1+r)^{-min(n,k)}}{r(1+r)^m} \\ \\\end{split}\]

Constraints¶

Retirement¶

The model computes the retirement of each technology and transmission line with these considerations:

  • The initial age of the technology and transmission line is based on its capacity ratio.

  • Each planning and scheduling period is based on the existing capacity.

The existing capacity for each year, in each zone, for each technology, is as follows:

\[\begin{split}cap_{y, z, te}^{existing-tech} = \sum_{lifetime-age<y-y_{min})}cap_{age,z,te}^{tech-init} + \sum_{(yy\le y) \& (lifetime>y-yy)}cap_{yy,z,te}^{tech-inv} \text{, for all } y,z,te \\ \\\end{split}\]

The existing capacity of the transmission lines for each year, from \(z_s\)-th zone to \(z_o\)-th zone, is as follows:

\[\begin{split}cap_{y, z_s, z_o}^{existing-line} = \sum_{lifetime-age<y-y_{min})}cap_{age,z_s,z_o}^{line-init} + \sum_{(yy\le y) \& (lifetime>y-yy)}cap_{yy,z_s,z_o}^{line-inv} \text{, for all } y,z_s,z_o \\ \\\end{split}\]

Carbon Emission¶

The model computes the carbon emissions for each year, based on the sum of carbon emissions from each zone, and from each technology.

The carbon emission for each technology, for each year, and in each zone, is as follows:

\[\begin{split}carbon_{y,te}^{tech} = \sum_{t,m,z}Carbon_{y,z,te}\times gen_{t,m,y,z,te} \quad \forall y,te \\ \\\end{split}\]

The carbon emission for each year is as follows:

\[\begin{split}carbon_{y} = \sum_{te}carbon_{y,te}^{tech} \forall y \\ \\\end{split}\]

Where, the calculated carbon emission for each year, must be lower than its upper bound, as follows:

\[\begin{split}carbon_{y} \le \overline{carbon}_y \forall y \\ \\\end{split}\]

Power Balance¶

The model computes the power balance for each hour, in each time period, for each year, and in each zone, as follows:

\[\begin{split}Demand_{t,m,y,z} = & \sum_{z_s\neq z}import_{t, m, y, z_s, z} - \sum_{z_o\neq z}export_{t, m, y, z, z_o} + \\ \\ & \sum_{te}gen_{t, m, y, z, te} - \sum_{te\in storage}charge_{t, m, y, z, te}\quad \forall t,m,y,te\end{split}\]

Transmission Loss¶

The model computes the transmission loss for each hour, in each time period, for each year, from \(z_s\)-th zone to \(z_o\)-th zone, as follows:

\[\begin{split}export_{t, m, y, z_s, z_o} \times Effi_{z_s, z_o}^{trans} = import_{t, m, y, z_s, z_o} \quad \forall t,y,z_s\neq z_o \\ \\\end{split}\]

Maximum Output¶

The model computes the maximum output for each hour, in each time period, for each year, in each zone, and for each technology, as follows:

\[\begin{split}gen_{t, m, y, z, te} \leq cap_{y, z, te}^{existing-tech} \forall t,m \\ \\\end{split}\]

Energy Storage¶

The model computes the energy storage level for each hour, for each year, in each zone, and for each technology, as follows:

\[\begin{split}storage_{t,y,z,te}^{level} = storage_{t-1,y,z, te}^{level} - \frac{gen_{t,y,z,te}}{Effi_{y,te}^{storage}} \quad \forall te \in storage, t,y,z \\ \\\end{split}\]

Where, the starting energy storage level is set to the initial storage level, as follows:

\[\begin{split}storage_{t,y,z,te}^{level} = Storage_{z, te}^{init} \quad \forall t,y=INI,z \\ \\\end{split}\]

And the final energy storage level is set to the ending storage level, as follows:

\[\begin{split}storage_{t,y,z}^{level} = Storage_{z, te}^{end} \quad \forall t,y=END,z \\ \\\end{split}\]

Ramping Ratio¶

The model computes the generated power and ensures it is less than the product of the ramping ratio and the existing capacity of each technology.

Where, the upper bound of the generated power is defined, as follows:

\[\begin{split}gen^{up}_{t,m,y,z,te} \le R^{up}_{te}\times cap_{y,z,te}^{existing-tech} \quad \forall t,m,y,z,te \\ \\\end{split}\]

And the lower bound of the generated power is defined, as follows:

\[\begin{split}gen^{down}_{t,m,y,z,te} \le R^{down}_{te}\times cap_{y,z,te}^{existing-tech} \quad \forall t,m,y,z,te \\ \\\end{split}\]

Finally, the difference between the upper and lower bound of the generated power, in the current hour, is equal to the difference between the generated power in the current hour and the previous hour, as follows:

\[\begin{split}gen^{up}_{t,m,y,z,te} - gen^{down}_{t,m,y,z,te} = gen_{t,m,y,z,te} - gen_{t-1,m,y,z,te} \quad \forall t,m,y,z,te \\ \\\end{split}\]
